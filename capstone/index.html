Solo Traveling

Inside all of us there is a strong desire to explore the world. Once that desire kicks in, there is no turning back...

Break the routine. Discover new horizons.

Everyone enjoys traveling. Vacations are often associated with positive emotional experiences. Experiences boost happiness and traveling does it from the very moment you start planning and the happy memories will linger long after you've returned home. Whether you're a normal tourist on a family holiday, traveling for business, or a back-packer ready for the adventure, travel not only breaks the routine but it also has the ability to change your perspective and understandings of the world, see beautiful new landscapes, meet new people from different cultures, and even build your self-confidence. In a single sentence: Travel makes you happier.

Wanderlust. Enhance your travel.

Once you've felt the positive emotional experiences of traveling you just don't want to stop feeling that way. Then is when you realize that not everyone has the same level of urge for traveling. Wanderlust is a personality trait and it's believed to have a genetic predisposition for it. Some studies suggest that higher levels of wanderlust are associated with greater odds of experiencing higher intensities of awe and joy during leisure travel. Thus, shaping the quality of travel experiences.

Travel on your own. Adventure awaits.

At fist glance, it might be frightening to go somewhere far and unknown on your own, facing unforeseen challenges with no one else to fall back on but yourself. But the desire for experiencing the unknown adventure is exactly what drives Wanderlust. If you've never traveled solo yet but love to travel, chances are, you will end up giving in to Wanderlust. And after a while, you will also start to feel like you need to get away from wherever you are to go somewhere else. Some may even say you don't travel to reach a destination but to escape from where you are. But no, you're not really running away, you're running towards. Truth is, travel and wanderlust are not about finding happinness in going places, they're about finding happinness in self-development, learning and appreciating beauty in awe.
